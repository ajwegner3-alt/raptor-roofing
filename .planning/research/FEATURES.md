## Anti-Features (Explicitly Exclude)

Features that actively hurt conversion or damage brand positioning for Raptor's specific context.

| Anti-Feature | Why Avoid | What to Do Instead |
|--------------|-----------|-------------------|
| Email-only contact form | Contractors are phone businesses; homeowners with urgent storm needs won't wait for email replies | Phone as primary field; email optional |
| "How did you hear about us?" field | Every additional required field costs ~27% abandonment; this question is useful for the client but not the customer | Capture source via UTM parameters in form metadata instead |
| Multi-step form with 3+ pages | Roofing leads are high-intent; friction at conversion time sends them to the next Google result | Single-page form, max 5 fields |
| Autoplay video with sound | Drives immediate bounce on mobile, especially in shared environments; degrades perceived professionalism | If using video, mute by default, autoplay only when user reaches section |
| Image slider/carousel in hero | Studies consistently show carousels reduce CTR vs static hero; the second slide is almost never seen | Single strong hero image with real photo |
| Aggressive popup within 3 seconds of load | For trust-led positioning, popups undermine the "we're professional neighbors" brand promise | Exit-intent offer or scroll-triggered after 50% page depth |
| Generic stock photography (white-gloved roofers, fake job sites) | Destroys the "real local crew" positioning that is Raptor's core differentiator | Use Raptor's own job site photos; placeholders labeled clearly for client replacement |
| Separate "About Us" page burying differentiators | "No subs, 15 years, family-operated" are conversion drivers — hiding them behind a navigation click loses the sale | Integrate key differentiators into hero micro-trust line and proof strip |
| Live chat that's obviously a bot | Breaks the "personal, family business" brand promise when the "owner" has a 0.3-second response time at 2am | No chat, or text-first contact (SMS link) if client wants async communication |
| Long-form "about our values" copy in hero | Homeowners scan; they don't read; hero copy above fold should be under 20 words total | Tight headline + micro-trust line + CTA |
| Multiple competing CTAs (3+) above fold | Decision paralysis reduces overall conversion | Two and only two above-fold CTAs: call + estimate |
| Aggressive CAPTCHA on lead form | reCAPTCHA v2 (checkbox or image puzzles) abandon rate is measurable; harms mobile UX | reCAPTCHA v3 (invisible) or Cloudflare Turnstile |
| Footer-only phone number placement | By the time they reach footer, the page has already failed to convert them | Phone in sticky header AND in hero AND after each major section |
| Service area pages with identical content ("just swap the city name") | Google detects and penalizes thin duplicate content; also fails the homeowner who lives in that city | Genuinely localized content per city — at minimum 2-3 paragraphs unique per page |

## Storm / Insurance UX (Special Focus)

Omaha is deep in the hail belt. Storm-damage customers are a distinct buyer persona from planned-project buyers. Raptor's "anti-chaser" positioning is the most defensible differentiator in this crowded market.

### The Omaha Storm-Damage Buyer Journey

After a hail event, a homeowner's decision sequence is:
1. Notice damage (or neighbor mentions it, or storm chaser knocks)
2. Search "roof damage Omaha" or "roof insurance claim Omaha"
3. Look for legitimacy signals within 15 seconds of landing
4. Decide: file a claim on my own, call this contractor, or keep shopping

The storm chaser problem is real and well-documented in the Omaha market. GW Contracting, Preferred Roofing, and McCoy Roofing all use some form of anti-chaser language. The market is noisy. Raptor needs to signal *permanence* and *process* at every touchpoint.

### Required Storm/Insurance UX Features

| Feature | Rationale | Complexity | Priority |
|---------|-----------|------------|----------|
| Dedicated Storm Damage page (not just a section) | Separate URL enables ranking for "storm damage roofing Omaha" and "hail damage roof Omaha" queries | Moderate | Must have |
| "Free Storm Inspection" as a standalone CTA (distinct from Free Estimate) | Emergency buyers need a different offer; inspection is lower commitment than estimate | Trivial | Must have |
| "Same-crew, no subcontractors" as explicit storm-damage trust signal | Directly rebuts the chaser model where unknown workers show up | Trivial | Must have |
| 3-step claims process explainer (visual diagram): Inspection → We Meet Your Adjuster → Repair | Anchor Roofing uses this effectively; removes process anxiety; positions Raptor as guide | Moderate | Must have |
| "We advocate for you with your insurance company" language | Homeowners fear being under-claimed; this promise converts skeptical buyers | Trivial | Must have |
| Emergency tarping section with 24/7 CTA | Already a Raptor service; needs its own page with urgency language and immediate phone display | Moderate | Must have |
| Anti-chaser explainer ("What to look for in a storm contractor") | McCoy Roofing has a blog post on this; a page on Raptor's site becomes a trust anchor and SEO asset | Moderate | Recommended |
| Insurance carrier logo row (State Farm, Allstate, Farmers, Auto-Owners, USAA) | Signals experience with major insurers; reduces homeowner anxiety about claim compatibility | Trivial | Recommended |
| "No money upfront until insurance approves" reassurance | Common storm-damage objection; addresses it proactively | Trivial | Recommended |
| Hail damage photo gallery with labeled damage types | Educational + social proof combo; rarely done well by Omaha competitors | Moderate | V2 |

### Storm UX Tone: Urgency vs. Trust Balance

The Omaha storm-chaser market is saturated with red-flag tactics (door-knocking, pressure timelines, "sign today" discounts). Raptor's anti-chaser positioning requires the opposite tone:

- **Avoid:** "Don't wait — hail season is here! Act NOW before your claim expires!"
- **Use:** "Take your time. We'll be here. We've been in Omaha 15 years and we're not leaving."
- **Avoid:** Insurance carrier logos used as "we'll handle everything, you don't have to do a thing" (power-of-attorney language that signals chaser tactics)
- **Use:** "We'll walk alongside you through the process" (partnership language, not takeover language)
- **CTA for storm page:** "Schedule a Free Damage Inspection" — not "File Your Claim Now"

## Accessibility Must-Haves

Roofing homeowners skew 35-65 years old. Older homeowners have higher rates of contrast sensitivity, motor difficulties, and screen reader use. WCAG 2.1 Level AA is the legal standard and the right target.

| Feature | Why It Matters for This Audience | Complexity | Priority |
|---------|----------------------------------|------------|----------|
| Minimum 4.5:1 contrast ratio on all body text | Older homeowners (40-65) are the primary buyer; contrast failures are most common a11y failure in contractor sites | Trivial | Must have |
| Tap targets minimum 44x44px (48x48 preferred) for all CTAs and phone links | Mobile contractor users often call immediately; undersized tap targets cause misdials and frustration | Trivial | Must have |
| Form error messaging with field-level indicators (not just "form failed") | Required for WCAG 3.3.1; also reduces form abandonment by 22% when errors are clearly labeled | Trivial | Must have |
| Skip-to-main-content link (screen reader navigation) | WCAG 2.4.1 requirement; hidden unless focused | Trivial | Must have |
| All images have descriptive alt text | Required by WCAG 1.1.1; also feeds image SEO | Trivial | Must have |
| No color-only information conveyance (e.g., required fields shown in red only) | Red-green colorblindness affects ~8% of males; use asterisk + color | Trivial | Must have |
| Keyboard navigation for all interactive elements (forms, accordions, menus) | Required for WCAG 2.1.1; also ensures tab-order is logical for form completion | Moderate | Must have |
| Focus-visible state on all interactive elements | WCAG 2.4.7; often removed by CSS resets accidentally | Trivial | Must have |
| No content that flashes more than 3 times per second | Photosensitivity; also just good design | Trivial | Must have |
| Font size minimum 16px for body text | iOS below 16px triggers auto-zoom, breaking layout; 16px+ improves readability for older users | Trivial | Must have |
